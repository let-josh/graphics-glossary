A sprite is a 2D flat plane that always faces the camera. By swapping out the
texture, the illusion of a 3D model can be achieved while keeping the geometry
extremely simple. This strategy was very common in the early days of 3D video
game development.

## Mario Kart 64

![a screenshot of the banshee boardwalk course from mario kart 64](@assets/banshee-boardwalk.png)

Mario Kart 64 makes heavy use of the billboarding technique. All of the racers
in the game are actually 2D sprites whose texture is dependent on the way the
racer is being steered. This was probably a necessary simplification
considering the development team's lack of experience with 3D game development
and the desire for the game to support up to 4 players.

### Sprites

The racers aren't the only sprites in the game. Many background elements such
as trees and enemies are also sprites. Some of these entities only have
"front-facing" spritesheets, however the spritesheet for the boos in _Banshee
Boardwalk_ give a "360-view" of the entity.

![an example of a spritesheet with views of an abalone shell mesh](@assets/abalone-spritesheet-16.png)

<App client:visible />

As the camera moves around the sprite, we see different angles of the sprite
"scene". It gives the semblance of a 3D model but in reality, a 2D plane is
being used and the texture is sampled in such a way to show the correct sprite.
